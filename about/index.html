What is this?

A job application that built its own page.

The page you just came from was generated by FORA — an open-source pipeline that turns a job description into a personalised application landing page.

How it works

You paste in a job URL. FORA fetches the description, runs a structured brainstorm against a designer's profile, and produces a content brief. The brief drives a code generator that fills in section templates — hero, proof of work, a 15/30/90-day commitment plan, a direct CTA. The result is a single HTML file, deployed to a unique URL.

Every page is written specifically for one company and one role. Nothing is reused between applications.

01Paste a job URL into the terminal
02A brainstorm agent analyses the JD and proposes positioning, proof of work, and 90-day commitments
03You review and refine in any AI chat — lock the brief when it's right
04FORA generates the page and deploys it to a live URL
05Send the URL in your cold message
Why it exists

Most job applications look identical. A resume, a cover letter, a portfolio link. FORA is a bet that a purpose-built page — written for a specific company, showing specific decisions, committing to a specific plan — is a stronger signal than any of those.

It's also a demonstration in itself. If you're hiring a designer who builds with AI, this is what that looks like.
